## Relative constructions

### Architecture
The chapter begins well, with a concrete learner's question and a governing principle: relative constructions are defined syntactically by the relation among antecedent, relative expression, and gap. From there, however, the chapter's route becomes less linear than its opening promises. It announces a taxonomy of relative types early, but does not walk through that taxonomy in one sustained sequence; instead it interposes processing difficulty, semantic restriction, supplements, clefts, and resumptives before returning to the remaining types. The chapter does eventually cover most of what the learning objectives promise, but the intellectual spine is intermittently obscured, so the reader must reconstruct the plan more often than the chapter should require.

### Structural flags
1. **§19.2 / Line ~67** [ARC]: The chapter declares a two-part taxonomy, basic and focused, with three focused subtypes, but it does not then proceed through that structure in order. Articulated relatives are treated at length, then the discussion breaks away for several sections before infinitival and fused relatives finally appear; the map is given early, but the route does not honor it.
2. **§19.2.2 / Line ~163** [TRANSITION]: The subsection on processing difficulty arrives before the descriptive taxonomy is complete. Because it is a cross-cutting issue rather than a type of relative construction, it would land more firmly after the major clause types have been established, or it needs an explicit signal that the chapter is pausing classification to discuss usage pressure.
3. **§19.2.3 / Line ~296** [SCAFFOLDING]: The restriction that basic relatives cannot be supplementary is stated before the chapter has fully built the integrated/supplementary distinction on which that claim depends. The reader meets the prohibition before being given the conceptual frame that makes it intelligible.
4. **§19.4 / Line ~359** [REDUNDANCY]: The section on supplementary relatives partly restarts work already done in the semantic-restriction section: modifier versus supplement, restrictive versus non-restrictive force, and the exclusion of basic relatives from supplementary use. Its genuinely new material, especially the widening of possible antecedents, needs to be marked more clearly as an extension.
5. **§19.5 / Line ~382** [ARC]: The section on it-clefts is a useful border case, but placed here it contributes to a miscellaneous middle. Since the announced account of focused types is still unfinished, the chapter's forward motion weakens just when it should be completing its central classification.
6. **§19.6 / Line ~411** [TRANSITION]: Resumptive pronouns are pedagogically important, but structurally they belong with other edge phenomena or applications, not between the main taxonomy and the deferred discussion of the remaining focused types. Their current placement makes the chapter feel as though it has temporarily changed subjects.
7. **§19.7.2 / Line ~488** [SCAFFOLDING]: Fused relatives significantly qualify the opening model, because they lack the separate antecedent-plus-relative-phrase architecture that the introduction presents as central. The chapter needs earlier foreshadowing that the opening account describes the default pattern, not the only one.
8. **§19.8 / Line ~520** [PAYOFF]: Recursive embedding is interesting, but the chapter has not been steering toward recursion as a culminating insight. It reads as an appended curiosity rather than as the next necessary step in the argument.
9. **§19.9 / Line ~553** [TRANSITION]: The phrase-structure section arrives so late that it feels like a second, diagrammatic chapter attached to the first. Since much of the earlier discussion depends on structural notions such as fronting, attachment, and fusion, either some diagrams should appear sooner or this section should be explicitly framed as retrospective consolidation.
10. **§19.10 / Line ~657** [PAYOFF]: The summary is full, but the promised diagnostic work remains dispersed rather than synthesized. The learning objectives suggest that the reader will finish with a usable procedure for analyzing relatives; the chapter more often offers local tests and contrasts than a settled analytic sequence.

### What holds
1. **§19.1** [STRONG]: The opening Reddit example gives the chapter a real problem to solve, and the introduction extracts from it the governing syntactic principle rather than leaving the chapter at the level of folk rules about `where`, `that`, and place meaning.
2. **§19.2.1** [STRONG]: The analogy with focused interrogatives is an effective piece of scaffolding. It recruits a structure the book has already taught and makes articulated relatives feel like part of a larger syntactic system rather than an isolated topic.
3. **§19.3** [STRONG]: The reframing of "restrictive/non-restrictive" as a relation between syntactic integration and semantic force is the chapter's best conceptual payoff. It corrects a common classroom simplification and strengthens the point by extending it beyond relative clauses themselves.
