## Some key concepts, terms, and ways of thinking about English grammar

### Architecture
The chapter begins with a genuine intellectual problem: how one can define grammatical categories without being misled by easy slogans. Its opening movement, from teacherly anxiety to soup to necessary and sufficient conditions to counter-example, gives the reader both a question and a method. The structure weakens, however, once nouns and countability take over; what had been a chapter about ways of thinking becomes, for a long stretch, a chapter about one especially rich noun problem. By the end, the reader has learned a great deal, but not always within a proportioned design: some parts are fully built, others are only sketched, and the inserted glossary interrupts the load-bearing line.

### Structural flags
1. **§0 / Line ~5** [PAYOFF]: The learning objectives promise identification of nouns, verbs, adjectives, and determinatives, along with an understanding of prototype-based lexical categories. The chapter substantially delivers nouns and, more briefly, verbs; adjectives and determinatives appear mostly as supporting terms, not as achieved objects of knowledge. Either narrow the objectives or add a closing synthesis that explicitly cashes out those promises.
2. **§1.3 / Line ~145** [TRANSITION]: The move from failed denotational definitions to the technical meaning of *phrase* is necessary, but the chapter does not quite say why it is necessary at that moment. The missing middle step is that category claims become explanatorily useful only when words are seen as heads of larger structures. Without that bridge, the section feels less like the next rung in an argument than like a new topic introduced on trust.
3. **§2 / Line ~195** [SCAFFOLDING]: The noun diagnostics are offered before the chapter has securely established several of the concepts they depend on: head, noun phrase, adjective phrase, adverb phrase, subject, object, possessive. Some forward reference is inevitable in grammar, but here too many criteria arrive as promissory notes at once. A novice reader is asked to accept the architecture before seeing the supports.
4. **§2.2 / Line ~320** [ARC]: The long discussion of countability and plurality is strong in itself, but it becomes the chapter's dominant mass. Because it absorbs so much argumentative energy, the chapter's announced subject, key concepts and ways of thinking, gives way to a detailed noun case study that dwarfs the later treatment of verbs. The material either needs to be framed explicitly as the chapter's central demonstration or redistributed so that the chapter keeps its original proportion.
5. **§3 / Line ~520** [TRANSITION]: The glossary appears in the middle of the chapter, between the noun-number discussion and the move to verbs, and so breaks the argumentative spine. The later "Glossary (continued)" confirms that this is apparatus rather than progression. As placed, it stops the chapter from carrying its reader forward.
6. **§4.1 / Line ~675** [SCAFFOLDING]: The auxiliary-verb section depends on terms and systems not yet built out here: interrogatives, perfect, modal status, even the full force of the NICER diagnostics. The instruction to "cycle back" is candid, but structurally it amounts to admitting that the section arrives before its explanatory prerequisites. It reads as a preview from a later chapter rather than the next necessary step in this one.
7. **§5 / Line ~715** [ARC]: The chapter opens with the soup question as its governing emblem, but the summary does not return to it in order to close the argument. The reader can infer the answer, that linguistic categories are better approached through families of criteria and counter-examples than through strict necessary-and-sufficient definitions, but the chapter should state that resolution outright. An opening question this prominent deserves an explicit final answer.

### What holds
1. **§1.0-§1.2** [STRONG]: The opening sequence has real architectural discipline. It starts from the reader's practical anxiety, introduces a seemingly trivial classificatory problem, and then enlarges that problem into a method for thinking about grammar. That is the beginning of a chapter with a discernible arc, not merely a list of topics.
2. **§2.2** [STRONG]: The countability discussion, especially the cross-linguistic and historical material, does genuine structural work for the chapter's larger claim. It shows that the habit of seeking counter-examples is not a classroom gimmick but a way of seeing where apparently obvious categories break down. In other words, the chapter's method is not only announced but enacted.
3. **§4** [STRONG]: The movement from verb to verb phrase to clause gives the reader a useful hierarchy and helps prevent lexical categories from being mistaken for sentence functions. Even in compressed form, this is the right kind of enlargement: from word, to phrase, to clause, with each level depending on the one before.
