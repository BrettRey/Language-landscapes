## Verbs, Tense, and Aspect

### Architecture
This chapter has an intelligible spine, but chiefly in its first half. It begins well: verb classes lead naturally to NICER diagnostics, and those diagnostics prepare the discussion of forms. After that, the governing claim about English tense arrives too late, so the reader must work through aspect and perfect constructions before receiving the distinction that should organize them. The final move to complementation contains strong material, but it reads less like culmination than like a second chapter attached to the first.

### Structural flags
1. **§5.5 / Line ~260** [SCAFFOLDING]: The chapter undertakes its full account of aspect before it has established the distinction between grammatical tense and semantic time. In the perfect section especially, the reader is asked to reason with reference time, past/present labels, and contrasts with the simple past using categories whose reinterpretation comes only later in §5.7. The argument therefore requires retroactive repair.
2. **§5.4 / Line ~190** [ARC]: The boxed discussion of gerunds is too large a detour at this point in the chapter. It opens a separate argument about category and function, and even about competing analyses, before the chapter has secured its main movement from verb forms to tense and aspect. What should be a landing becomes a side corridor.
3. **§5.6-§5.7 / Line ~450** [TRANSITION]: The cross-linguistic section ends by asking how many tenses English has, precisely the question on which the chapter should pivot. But the answer is delayed by an exercise, and the next section restarts rather than completes the movement. A structural hinge is treated as an interruption.
4. **§5.8 / Line ~560** [REDUNDANCY]: “The English tense/aspect system” partly recapitulates work already done in the Aspect and Tense and Time sections, yet it is not framed as a summary. Because it also introduces new material on modality and non-finite forms, the section has an uncertain function. The reader cannot tell whether this is culmination, recap, or new departure.
5. **§5.8 / Line ~600** [PAYOFF]: The learning objectives promise an explanation of how modal auxiliaries express modality, but modality receives no treatment architecturally equivalent to the chapter’s treatment of aspect. It appears late, folded into remarks on remoteness and defective paradigms, so the reader gets observations without a governing map. The promise is met in pieces rather than as an argument.
6. **§5.9 / Line ~735** [TRANSITION]: The move from the tense/aspect system to verb complementation is abrupt. Complement selection matters, but the chapter has not prepared the reader to see why valency should follow the temporal and auxiliary systems here rather than in a separate chapter on clause structure. The material is sound; its placement is insufficiently motivated.
7. **§5.9.1 / Line ~820** [GAP]: “Auxiliary Verbs as Heads” supplies a principle that could have unified much of the chapter much earlier. Had the reader been given this framing sooner, NICER, aspect formation, modal licensing, and non-finite verb forms would have belonged to one visible architecture. As placed, the section explains retrospectively what should have organized prospectively.

### What holds
1. **§5.2-§5.4** [STRONG]: The opening sequence from lexical versus auxiliary verbs to NICER diagnostics to verb forms is well ordered. Each section answers a question raised by the previous one: what the classes are, how auxiliaries differ, and what forms those verbs can take. That is genuine scaffolding.
2. **§5.7** [STRONG]: Once the chapter arrives at tense versus time, the argument becomes sharp and consequential. The two-tense analysis does more than correct terminology; it gives the reader a principle for reorganizing familiar textbook material. This is the chapter’s clearest conceptual center, even if it appears late.
3. **§5.9.1** [STRONG]: The claim that auxiliaries are heads, not merely “helping verbs,” is the chapter’s best late payoff. It converts a set of earlier observations into a structural explanation and ties auxiliaries back to complementation in a satisfying way.
