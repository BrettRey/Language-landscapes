## Questions and interrogatives

### Architecture
This chapter has a serviceable local order but an unsettled overall arc. It opens as if its governing question will be what questions do in classrooms, then spends most of its weight on the syntax of interrogative clauses, then appears to conclude before beginning again with classroom application and phrase structure. The reader can follow each component, but the chapter has two centers of gravity rather than one. Its best stretches are cumulative in sequence; its larger design does not always make clear how those sequences answer the chapter's opening problem.

### Structural flags
1. **§10.1 / Line ~30** [ARC]: The opening classroom vignette establishes a pragmatic problem: what is achieved by asking questions in class, and what is not. Once the chapter turns to the syntax of interrogatives, that problem largely disappears until §10.6. The result is a weakly joined opening and body, as if the chapter begins by promising one inquiry and then conducts another.
2. **§10.3 / Line ~91** [TRANSITION]: `Main-clause interrogatives` should be the hinge that tells the reader what territory the chapter will cover and why this division follows from the question/interrogative distinction. Instead it names the two types and moves on. Because the scope is not firmly stated here, later material on declarative questions, echo questions, and subordinate interrogatives feels less like controlled boundary-setting than like afterthought.
3. **§10.5.3 / Line ~348** [SCAFFOLDING]: This section asks the reader to work with `Pre` function, fronting, and gaps before the phrase-structure section has built those concepts. The cross-reference to a later explanation acknowledges the delay but does not remove it. The architecture should not require the reader to borrow machinery from a section not yet reached.
4. **§10.5.5 / Line ~509** [ARC]: The subsection called `Summary`, especially with the decision tree, feels like the conclusion of the chapter's formal argument. But substantial sections still remain. This creates premature closure and leaves the later classroom and phrase-structure material sounding appended rather than consequential.
5. **§10.7 / Line ~593** [PAYOFF]: Tree drawing is one of the stated learning objectives, yet phrase structure arrives only after the practical classroom section, when the chapter already seems to have cashed out its pedagogical relevance. That placement makes the trees feel supplementary instead of load-bearing. If they are meant to clarify the earlier account of gaps and inversion, they need to arrive earlier or be foreshadowed more explicitly as the culminating explanatory frame.
6. **§10.5.6 / Line ~531** [REDUNDANCY]: By this point the chapter has already established that interrogatives are not always questions and that form and function do not map one-to-one. Repeating the point again in the focused-interrogative semantics section does not advance the argument; it recycles a premise. The repetition would matter more if each return tightened or revised the chapter's system, but it mostly restates.
7. **§10.8 / Line ~688** [PAYOFF]: The final summary claims a more comprehensive architecture than the chapter has fully built. What is thoroughly organized here is the syntax of main-clause interrogatives, with some classroom consequences and peripheral question types. The title and conclusion imply a broader theory of questions than the chapter's actual structure securely supports.

### What holds
1. **§10.2** [STRONG]: The distinction between `question` and `interrogative` is the chapter's essential load-bearing beam. It gives the reader a durable principle early enough to support later exceptions and classroom cases.
2. **§10.4** [STRONG]: The section on basic interrogatives proceeds in a sound pedagogical order: inversion, the special status of auxiliaries, subject identification, then `do`-support, with semantics and intonation following form. That sequence lets each idea prepare the next.
3. **§10.5.2** [STRONG]: The move from interrogative phrase subjects to non-subject focus is the chapter's best piece of cumulative reasoning. It begins with the easy case, then complicates it in a way the reader can feel as necessary rather than ornamental.
4. **§10.6** [STRONG]: The return to classroom questions is intellectually justified, not merely topical. Here the earlier distinctions finally show their pedagogical value, especially in separating natural ellipsis from inappropriate declarative-question substitutes.
