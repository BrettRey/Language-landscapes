## Categories, functions, and constructions

### Architecture
This chapter has abundance but only intermittent shape. It begins deep inside the analysis of subjects before it has properly established the conceptual scheme promised by its title: category, function, and construction as distinct but related levels of description. The local sections are often sound in themselves, yet they do not always seem to know why they follow one another, so the chapter can read as a sequence of competent dossiers rather than a steadily unfolding argument. Only in the concluding pages does the underlying pedagogical design become fully visible, and by then the reader has had to infer the architecture retroactively.

### Structural flags
1. **§12.1 / Line ~33** [SCAFFOLDING]: The chapter opens with `subject` as though the reader already had a clear map of category, function, complement, and construction. Since those distinctions are the chapter's announced subject, the framework ought to be drawn before the first major case study begins.
2. **§12.1 / Line ~39** [ARC]: The long section on subjects accumulates position, category, denotation, passive behavior, case, licensing, agreement, and dropping, but it does not rank them. The reader is not told which are core diagnostics, which are common tendencies, and which are exceptional complications, so the section has the feel of a list rather than a progression.
3. **§12.1 / Line ~201** [TRANSITION]: `Subject dropping` should be the stress test of the previous discussion, showing what remains of subjecthood when overt expression disappears. Instead it arrives more as an appended pedagogical excursus, interesting in itself but weakly joined to the argument it ought to complete.
4. **§12.2 / Line ~289** [PAYOFF]: The determiner section begins by asking the reader to infer the system from examples and questions, which is a good architectural move. But the section then diverts into history and exposition without explicitly returning to answer those questions in order, so the initial promise is only partly redeemed.
5. **§12.3 / Line ~390** [GAP]: The treatment of objects is too brief for the amount of later work it must support. Because predicative complements, resultatives, and alternations all depend on a stable sense of what counts as an object, the chapter needs a firmer bridge from `object` to the wider complement system.
6. **§12.4 / Line ~432** [REDUNDANCY]: `Other complements` and `Modifiers` both spend energy establishing the complement-modifier contrast, but the second section largely restarts the argument instead of building from a settled framework. A single diagnostic matrix introduced once and then reused would give these two sections cumulative force.
7. **§12.6 / Line ~702** [TRANSITION]: The section on constructions has a much clearer governing idea than much of what precedes it: form-meaning pairings that cannot be reduced to the words alone. But that idea is not explicitly tied back to the earlier discussions of categories and functions, so the chapter's final movement feels appended rather than earned.

### What holds
1. **§12.1** [STRONG]: The refusal to define subject by any single criterion is architecturally right. Treating subjecthood as a cluster of properties prepares the reader for non-canonical cases and keeps the chapter from building on a false simplicity.
2. **§12.5** [STRONG]: The modifiers section has the clearest internal sequence in the chapter: definition, position, categories, stacking, then integrated versus supplementary status. Here the material genuinely develops rather than merely accumulates.
3. **§12.7** [STRONG]: The final three-level teaching sequence gives the chapter its best retrospective design. It clarifies how categories, functions, and constructions are meant to relate, even if that clarifying principle would serve the chapter better nearer the beginning.
