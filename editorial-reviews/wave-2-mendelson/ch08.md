## Analyzing vocabulary

### Architecture
The chapter has a clear and persuasive spine in its first half: it begins by asking what counts as a word, then moves from morphological structure to the units by which words are counted, and only then turns to frequency and coverage. That sequence gives the reader a genuine scaffold, because the later statistics depend on the earlier distinctions. After the coverage section, however, the chapter's governing question becomes less explicit: polysemy, collocation, idiom, syntax, and pedagogy are all pertinent, but their order feels additive rather than necessary. The result is a chapter with a strong opening architecture and a more weakly integrated final third.

### Structural flags
1. **§1 / Line ~36** [TRANSITION]: The introduction says the chapter will begin with morphemes and word formation, but the chapter in fact begins with the semantic and syntactic status of "word." Those are not the same opening move. Either the introduction should frame the definitional problem as the real first step, or the sequence should be adjusted to match the promise.
2. **§2.3 / Line ~103** [SCAFFOLDING]: The morphology practice asks readers to explain why *singer* and *singing* belong to different lemmas but the same word family before §2.4 has defined either *lemma* or *word family*. This asks the reader to use a distinction before the chapter has built it.
3. **§3.2 / Line ~241** [GAP]: The chapter moves from frequency ranks to claims about knowing 1,000, 5,000, or 8,000 families and reaching 98% coverage, but it does not pause to reassert that these are counts in word families rather than lemmas, shapes, or tokens. Since §2 has carefully taught those distinctions, §3 needed to cash them out more explicitly here.
4. **§3.3 / Line ~269** [TRANSITION]: Specialized vocabulary enters abruptly as a separate subsection. It is relevant, but the chapter does not show whether this is an application of the coverage argument, an exception to it, or a bridge to pedagogy. As placed, it feels inserted rather than structurally compelled.
5. **§4 / Line ~289** [ARC]: The shift from frequency and coverage to "Form, meaning, and use" is conceptually defensible, but the chapter never states the question that now governs the exposition. A short hinge such as "frequency tells us how much vocabulary matters; the next question is what it means to know a word" would make this a progression rather than a new list.
6. **§4.3 / Line ~324** [ARC]: The construction-grammar paragraph suddenly enlarges the chapter's definition of vocabulary from words to all stored form-meaning pairings. That is a major theoretical turn, but it arrives at the end of the idioms subsection and is not prepared earlier or taken up later. It reads as an imported framework rather than a developed consequence of the chapter's argument.
7. **§5.1 / Line ~345** [PAYOFF]: The chapter promises to show how complementation patterns and selectional restrictions link vocabulary to syntax, but this section is too brief to bear that weight. The point is asserted, not developed, and it is not subsequently harvested in the pedagogical section, where it could have shown why knowing a word includes knowing how it combines.

### What holds
1. **§2.1-§2.4** [STRONG]: The opening movement from "what is a word?" to morphology to families, lemmas, shapes, and tokens is the chapter's best piece of architecture. Each distinction creates the need for the next one, so the reader feels argument rather than accumulation.
2. **§3.1-§3.2** [STRONG]: The chapter does well to convert raw frequency facts into the more intelligible notion of text coverage. That gives the quantitative material a real payoff and prepares the reader for later claims about instructional priority.
3. **§6.1-§6.3** [STRONG]: The pedagogical pitfalls are strongest where they grow directly from the earlier argument about frequency and distribution. Here the chapter shows that its descriptive claims are not inert information but principles with consequences for what teachers choose to emphasize.
