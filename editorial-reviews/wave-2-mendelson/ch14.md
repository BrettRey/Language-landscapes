## Information packaging

### Architecture
The chapter has a sensible first movement: it begins with the governing intuition of given and new information, then refines that intuition through topic and focus, and only afterward turns to the constructions that realize these pressures in English. That is the right large-scale order. The weakness is in the second half, where the chapter becomes more catalogic than cumulative: the reader is shown one construction after another before being given a clear comparative framework for why these belong together. The chapter does have a shape, but its deepest claim, that these constructions form a system of choices for different discourse situations, is stated too late to govern the whole.

### Structural flags
1. **§14.1 / Line ~35** [ARC]: The introduction lists the chapter's topics, but it does not yet state the question that should govern the rest of the chapter: by what criteria are these constructions to be compared? Until that frame appears much later, the middle of the chapter risks reading as a toolbox inventory rather than an argument about choices.
2. **§14.1.2 / Line ~80** [SCAFFOLDING]: The move from given/new to topic/focus needs a sharper statement of difference. As written, the second subsection partly redescribes the first in new terminology before showing why the new terms are necessary; the reader is asked to carry two overlapping frameworks without being told how they divide the labor.
3. **§14.1.2 / Line ~97** [TRANSITION]: The detour into focused interrogatives and wh-movement depends on earlier syntactic material and briefly shifts the chapter from information packaging to question formation. A short bridge is needed to explain why this belongs here, otherwise the chapter momentarily loses its line of advance.
4. **§14.2 / Line ~143** [SCAFFOLDING]: The chapter needs a stronger pivot between discourse notions and the construction survey. Before the passive is introduced, the reader should be told that the remaining sections can be understood as solutions to recurring discourse problems such as topic continuity, contrastive focus, end-weight, and referent management.
5. **§14.2.3 / Line ~274** [GAP]: "Preposing and postposing" gathers together several marked orders whose family resemblance is not made explicit. Adjunct fronting, negative inversion, heavy shift, and clause-final modifiers do not all solve the same problem, so the section needs either clearer subdivision or an explicit rationale for treating them as one unit.
6. **§14.2.5 / Line ~466; §14.3 / Line ~492** [PAYOFF]: The sentence announcing that these constructions "form a system" arrives at the point where it ought to be cashing out an argument already made. The genre and pedagogy paragraph gives the chapter real payoff, but it also reveals that the promised applied skill, especially recognizing packaging breakdowns, has been less systematically developed than the learning objectives suggest.

### What holds
1. **§14.1.1-14.1.2** [STRONG]: The initial sequence from given/new to topic/focus is fundamentally sound. It starts with a processing principle a reader can grasp intuitively, then moves toward the more analytic vocabulary needed for the rest of the chapter.
2. **§14.2.1** [STRONG]: The passive section has the clearest internal architecture in the chapter. It does not simply define a form; it organizes several discourse motives for choosing it and repeatedly links those motives to pedagogical use.
3. **§14.2.4-14.2.5** [STRONG]: Placing extraposition immediately before right dislocation is a good local structural decision. The two constructions are superficially similar in sending material rightward, and the contrast between dummy and referential pronouns sharpens the distinction between them.
