## Preface

### Architecture
The preface has a recognizable spine: it introduces the book's scope, walks the reader through the chapter sequence, and explains why that sequence matters. Its central paragraph on order is the strongest piece of the design, because it gives the book an argument about progression rather than a mere table of contents. But the preface is not fully settled on which claim governs the others: the topical map, the challenge to inherited grammar, or the invitation to modular use. Because those claims are introduced in different places and not clearly ranked, the chapter has one solid middle but a less coherent overall arc.

### Structural flags
1. **Opening overview / Line ~3 and "The global market..." / Line ~18** [ARC]: The book's governing dispute with traditional grammar arrives only after the reader has already been led through the chapter map. Since this dispute is what justifies the book's distinctiveness and much of its sequence, it should be established near the beginning and made to govern the roadmap that follows.
2. **"This order is intentional..." / Line ~7 and "The chapters are more or less modular" / Line ~9** [GAP]: The preface first argues for a necessary conceptual progression and then immediately relaxes that progression into modularity. Both claims can stand, but the reader needs the missing middle term: which chapters are actual prerequisites, which are merely preferred sequences, and what is forfeited when the order is changed.
3. **"This book maps the terrain; it's up to you to explore it." / Line ~16** [TRANSITION]: This sentence behaves like a conclusion, but the preface continues with its most consequential theoretical framing. The result is a false ending that weakens the transition into the discussion of inherited grammar, CGEL, and the two closing notes.
4. **"Each chapter equips you..." / Line ~5 and "It also reaches well beyond grammar..." / Line ~18** [REDUNDANCY]: The expansion beyond grammar is announced twice, first as roadmap and later as differentiation from other textbooks, but the second occurrence does not build on the first into a sharper claim. One statement of scope and a separate statement of motive would make the structure cleaner.
5. **"A note about the grammatical framework" / Line ~20 and "A note about Canadian English" / Line ~24** [SCAFFOLDING]: These two notes are not architecturally equal. The framework note is central to how every later chapter should be read, while the Canadian English note is a local qualification affecting one domain. Placing them side by side at the end flattens their importance and delays the framework note beyond the point where it is most needed.

### What holds
1. **Roadmap and rationale / Lines ~5-7** [STRONG]: The preface does more than list chapters; it explains the movement from foundations to form, from form to patterns, and from patterns to use. That gives the book a genuine intellectual itinerary.
2. **"The chapters are more or less modular..." / Line ~9** [STRONG]: Once its relation to the default sequence is clarified, the alternative entry paths are a useful architectural feature. They translate the book's overall design into workable routes for readers with different aims.
3. **"A note about the grammatical framework" / Line ~20** [STRONG]: The preface rightly acknowledges that the book simplifies CGEL rather than reproducing it wholesale. That admission defines scope, prevents false expectations, and keeps the reader from mistaking a pedagogical adaptation for a complete theoretical system.
