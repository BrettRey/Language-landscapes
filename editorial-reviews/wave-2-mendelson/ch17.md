## Conversations

### Architecture
This chapter has a visible outer frame: an annotated opening conversation serves as specimen, the body of the chapter names the mechanisms that specimen contains, and the summary returns the reader to it. The difficulty lies in the middle architecture. The introduction promises a chapter about conversation as a self-organizing system, but the body often proceeds as a sequence of adjacent topics rather than as stages in that argument. The result is rich and useful, but its intellectual hierarchy is not always clear: the reader is sometimes asked to understand a consequence before the underlying mechanism, and some later sections feel appended rather than structurally prepared.

### Structural flags
1. **§17.1 / Line ~74** [ARC]: The introduction defines the governing idea as emergence and self-organization, but that principle is not then used to order the rest of the chapter. The middle reads more like a survey of phenomena than a progressive demonstration of how local adjustments scale up into conversational order.
2. **§17.2.3 / Line ~279** [SCAFFOLDING]: "Grammar in Interaction" arrives before §17.3 has explained turn-constructional units, projection, and recognizable completion. Collaborative completion therefore appears before the reader has been given the machinery that explains why another speaker can enter at precisely that point.
3. **§17.3 / Line ~332** [TRANSITION]: Only after a major section called "Beyond Turn-Taking" does the chapter fully explain how speakers know when a turn is ending. That reverses the explanatory sequence: the chapter asks the reader to move beyond a foundation that has not yet been firmly laid.
4. **§17.4 and §17.6.3 / Line ~424 and ~620** [REDUNDANCY]: Repair is first treated as part of grounding/common ground, then returns under "Side Sequences and Corrections" without a clear statement of the relation between the two. Are side sequences one form of repair, or a broader sequential category of which repair is one part? The chapter needs that hinge.
5. **§17.5 / Line ~521** [TRANSITION]: The boxed exercise on "Grammar as Real-Time Resource" sits inside "Bodies in Conversation." Because it directs the reader back to an earlier conceptual unit rather than forward within the present one, it breaks the section's internal continuity and gives the impression of materials not yet fully sorted.
6. **§17.6 / Line ~560** [ARC]: "Information and Action" begins as if the chapter is only now turning to what conversation does, yet earlier sections have already been discussing invitations, grounding, repair, and topic management as forms of action. Without a clearer statement that this section is a higher-order synthesis, it feels like a second beginning.
7. **§17.7 / Line ~645** [PAYOFF]: The learning objectives promise sustained attention to power, identity, and social relationships, but "Social Dimensions" is comparatively brief and late. Because those pressures are not woven through earlier sections, one of the chapter's announced stakes arrives as an appendix rather than as an organizing force.

### What holds
1. **§17.9** [STRONG]: The return to the opening dialogue in the summary and synthesis exercise gives the chapter real structural payoff. The reader is asked to go back to the specimen with analytic tools now in hand.
2. **§17.2.1** [STRONG]: The discussion of multiple timescales is the chapter's strongest piece of conceptual scaffolding. It offers a nested model roomy enough to hold pauses, turns, sequences, activities, and situations in a single frame.
3. **§17.4** [STRONG]: The common-ground section is well sequenced. It moves from parent-child exchange to adult colleagues to directions to repair, and each example enlarges the same claim about how mutual understanding is built.
4. **§17.8** [STRONG]: The digital section successfully translates earlier categories into a new medium. Typing indicators, explicit markers, and emoji are made to feel like structural variants of phenomena the chapter has already taught, not wholly new material.
