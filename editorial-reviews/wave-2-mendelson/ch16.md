## Pragmatics

### Architecture
This chapter has the materials of a coherent design, but not always the order that would let them lock together. It opens strongly by posing the central problem of pragmatics, then disperses that momentum by moving into pedagogy before the conceptual framework is fully assembled. The later sections on implicature, speech acts, politeness, context, and teaching are each intelligible, but they often sit beside one another rather than growing necessarily from one another. The result is broad coverage with intermittent shape: the reader learns the territory, but the chapter does not consistently show why this is the right path through it.

### Structural flags
1. **§16.1.1 / Line ~66** [ARC]: The chapter leaves its conceptual opening too early. Having just distinguished pragmatics from semantics, it turns to teacher misconceptions and Nation's Four Strands before the reader has been given the chapter's analytic instruments. That pedagogical urgency is understandable, but structurally it would work better either as a brief frame at the start or as a concluding application after the concepts have been built.
2. **§16.5 / Line ~437** [SCAFFOLDING]: "Context and Meaning" arrives after implicature, speech acts, and politeness, though all three sections have already depended on context as their silent premise. The chapter has been using the concept long before it organizes it. As a result, this section reads less like a necessary step forward than like a retroactive explanation of what earlier sections have already assumed.
3. **§16.2.1 / Line ~183** [PAYOFF]: The discussion of abduction supplies the chapter's most powerful explanation of how pragmatic interpretation works, but it is structurally buried as a subsubsection under implicature. Because it is not promoted as a governing principle, the later sections on speech acts, politeness, and context do not explicitly inherit it. The chapter finds its engine here, then does not clearly use that engine to drive the rest.
4. **§16.3 / Line ~221** [TRANSITION]: The move from implicature to speech acts needs an articulated bridge. The reader can infer the connection, but the chapter does not say why a discussion of implied meaning should now become a discussion of actions performed by utterances. A brief transition would make the chapter feel cumulative rather than sectional.
5. **§16.4.3 / Line ~366** [REDUNDANCY]: The later "Addressing the teacher" discussion revisits power, distance, imposition, and cross-cultural failure in a more local setting, but the chapter does not frame it as an application or culmination of the preceding subsection. Without that framing, it feels like a second start on material the reader has just been taught to analyze. The case itself is useful; the architecture needs to tell the reader why it is here.
6. **§16.6 / Line ~473** [TRANSITION]: The chapter returns to teaching after several theoretical sections, but it does not clearly signal that it is now cashing out the preceding analysis pedagogically. Because teaching was already foregrounded in §16.1.1, this later section does not feel like a deliberate destination so much as a resumed topic. The pedagogical thread should either frame the whole chapter more overtly or be reserved for the end.
7. **§16.6.1 / Line ~502** [PAYOFF]: The AI subsection arrives very late and introduces a tool-specific topic that the chapter has not prepared as part of its conceptual design. That shifts the ending away from a resolution of the chapter's main argument and toward an appendix-like coda. If AI is important to the book's pedagogy, it needs earlier signposting; otherwise it should be compressed or relocated.

### What holds
1. **§16.1** [STRONG]: The opening exchange and the "It's cold" set create a genuine organizing question: how does meaning exceed literal content? This gives the chapter a usable point of departure and a recurring test case for later concepts. The semantics/pragmatics contrast is placed where it belongs, at the threshold.
2. **§16.2** [STRONG]: The implicature section has a real internal progression: uncooperative exchanges, Gricean expectations, maxim-flouting, and then cross-cultural variation. It moves from intuition to framework without losing the reader's sense of the phenomenon being explained.
3. **§16.4** [STRONG]: The politeness section is the chapter's best-built structure. "Face" functions as a true organizing abstraction, and the movement from face-threatening acts to strategies to the variables of power, distance, and imposition gives the reader an analytic ladder rather than a list.
