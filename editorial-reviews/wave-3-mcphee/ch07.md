## Speech and the sound system

### Weight
This chapter is carrying more freight than one chapter quite has room for. The center of it earns the pages, but the chapter spends too much space on preview, throat-clearing, and side instruction while later large subjects arrive in outline form. It feels heavy at the front and thin at the back.

### Structural flags
1. **§1 / Line ~28** [RITUAL]: The introductory list repeats the learning objectives and the section headings. One roadmap is guidance; two are furniture.
2. **§2 / Line ~40** [LENGTH]: The section takes a long run at the point that speech is physically complex. The useful work begins once the chapter reaches the articulators.
3. **§2.1 / Line ~71** [DECORATIVE]: The uvula paragraph announces its own marginality by noting that it is uncommon in English. It can be cut without the reader feeling the floor move.
4. **§4 / Line ~241** [SEQUENCE]: Pronunciation goals matter, but here they interrupt the chapter between inventory and principle. This belongs nearer the front as a frame, or later as application.
5. **§5 / Line ~267** [LENGTH]: The strawberries analogy is sound, but it is overfunded. The section needs the distinction more than it needs this much runway.
6. **§5 / Line ~302** [RITUAL]: The boxed note on vowels and consonants is terminology housekeeping. It does not earn box space.
7. **§6.1 / Line ~343** [SEQUENCE]: The `-es` and `-ed` material is practical, but it is grafted onto vowel length. It would sit more naturally with phonotactics or in a teaching-application section.
8. **§8 / Line ~408** [LENGTH]: Stress, intonation, and rhythm are too consequential to be handled this briefly. In its current form, the section reads like a placeholder for a fuller chapter.
