## Understanding language proficiency

### Weight
As a chapter, this is about the right size. As appendix matter, it is misfiled: it argues, while the chapters around it mainly inventory.

### Structural flags
1. **§A / Line ~1** [SEQUENCE]: This belongs in the main run of the book, not in back matter. It asks the reader to think, not to look something up.
2. **§A.2 / Line ~24** [DECORATIVE]: The James Scott frame is a piece of furniture. The chapter can make its case through CLB and CEFR without borrowing a larger theory.
3. **§A.3 / Line ~66** [LENGTH]: "What gets lost" is strong, but it makes one structural point three times. Two losses would likely carry the load.
4. **§A.5 / Line ~86** [DECORATIVE]: The conclusion repeats the work of "Using frameworks wisely." It closes the door twice.

### Load-bearing
1. **§A.1** [EARNS IT]: The opening cases break the idea of a single proficiency level before the chapter starts naming frameworks.
2. **§A.2.1-§A.2.2** [EARNS IT]: CLB and CEFR give the critique something concrete to press against; without them the chapter stays abstract.
3. **§A.4** [EARNS IT]: This is where the chapter becomes usable for teachers. Without it, the piece remains diagnosis only.

## List of Prepositions

### Weight
Too heavy for the work it does in print. Its existence makes sense as reference matter, but its full acreage feels better suited to a supplement than to pages in the book.

### Structural flags
1. **§B.1 / Line ~1** [LENGTH]: After the prefatory sentence, the appendix shifts from evidence to accumulation. The reader learns the point early and then turns pages through proof.
2. **§B.1 / Line ~1** [RITUAL]: This feels partly included because comprehensive grammars are expected to have a list like this. Unless the main chapters repeatedly send readers back here, it is furniture in book form.

### Load-bearing
1. **§B.1** [EARNS IT]: A specimen list is necessary because CGEL's preposition class is one of the book's recurring surprises. The appendix shows the category is broader than school grammar says.

## List of determinatives

### Weight
Shorter than the preposition list, but even less clearly needed. It feels like a second appendix built from the same template after the first template has already made its point.

### Structural flags
1. **§C.1 / Line ~1** [RITUAL]: A second lexical inventory in immediate succession begins to look procedural. The appendix starts furnishing a room the reader has already entered.
2. **§C.1 / Line ~3** [SEQUENCE]: The note about numerals doing double duty is the one live complication here, and it wants to live near the determinatives chapter, not be buried in back matter.

### Load-bearing
1. **§C.1** [EARNS IT]: The brief statement of what counts as a determinative under CGEL gives the reader a reference point. That function is real, though small.

## Ordered list of teachable graphemes

### Weight
Long, but more justified than the two lexical inventories. This appendix is a tool with a clear pedagogical use, though the uninterrupted table could do more structural work if it were broken into stages.

### Structural flags
1. **§D.1 / Line ~1** [SEQUENCE]: This is the most classroom-ready appendix. It may deserve to appear before the category lists, or be signaled more prominently, because it answers a practical need at once.
2. **§D.1 / Line ~5** [LENGTH]: The table earns length, but 122 uninterrupted rows flatten priority into sequence. Some internal staging would make the bulk work harder.

### Load-bearing
1. **§D.1** [EARNS IT]: The opening paragraphs establish the selection rule: frequent, reliable, teachable. That keeps the table from becoming a dump.
2. **§D.1** [EARNS IT]: The ordered list itself is the appendix's real instrument. Unlike the lexical inventories, this one directly supports action.
