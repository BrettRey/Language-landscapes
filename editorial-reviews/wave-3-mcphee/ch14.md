## Information packaging

### Weight
This chapter is slightly over fighting weight. Its real beam is simple and strong: English normally moves from given to new, and the named constructions matter when they bend that order for a reason. After that beam is in place, the chapter keeps adding pieces of furniture.

### Structural flags
1. **§14.1 / Line ~26** [LENGTH]: The opening clears its throat too long. Definition, suitcase metaphor, and pedagogical justification all do the same work; one of them is enough before the chapter begins in earnest.
2. **§14.1.1 / Line ~69** [RITUAL]: The "Check Your Understanding" box asks for immediate paraphrase of a point already made plainly. It adds pages, not pressure.
3. **§14.1.2 / Line ~98** [LENGTH]: Topic and focus belong here, but the subsection wanders into focused interrogatives and previously covered machinery. Keep only the distinction the later sections actually need.
4. **§14.2.2 / Line ~255** [LENGTH]: Clefts get more acreage than their payoff warrants. The chapter later admits they are low-return for teaching; the structure should believe its own judgment.
5. **§14.2.3 / Line ~320** [LENGTH]: Preposing and postposing become a specimen cabinet. After a few examples, the principle is established; the rest is display.
6. **§14.2.4 / Line ~395** [SEQUENCE]: Extraposition is one of the chapter's clearest, most usable cases, yet it arrives after the broader and less central fronting/backing survey. It should come earlier, while the beam is still visible.
7. **§14.3 / Line ~540** [RITUAL]: The summary re-lists the taxonomy after a chapter already inclined to list-making. It does not close the piece; it restates it.

### Load-bearing
1. **§14.1.1** [EARNS IT]: Given vs new is the chapter's law of motion. Without it, every later construction is merely rearrangement.
2. **§14.2.1** [EARNS IT]: The passive section makes the subject concrete. It shows packaging doing real discourse work: topic continuity, agent suppression, end-weight.
3. **§14.2.4** [EARNS IT]: Extraposition produces a practical editorial test. It translates abstraction into something a teacher can hear in a student sentence and fix.
4. **§14.2** [EARNS IT]: The late "one proposition, seven packages" exercise earns its space because it finally puts the constructions under pressure side by side. Here the chapter stops naming parts and starts showing choice.
