## Categories, functions, and constructions

### Weight
This chapter carries two loads: the diagnostics of category and function, and a compact survey of constructions. Either can bear a chapter. Together they make the piece feel heavy and uneven, because `Subjects` and `Constructions` are expansive while `Objects` is scarcely a room and parts of `Determiners` and `Modifiers` read like attachments.

### Structural flags
1. **Front matter / Line ~5** [RITUAL]: The paired opening boxes announce the chapter twice. One frame is enough; the second is lobby furniture before the chapter has started.
2. **§1.1 / Line ~47** [LENGTH]: The subject section keeps proving subjecthood after it has been proved. Position, category, passive behavior, case, licensing, and agreement all matter, but in one run they overbuild the room.
3. **§2 / Line ~317** [FURNITURE]: The brief history of determiners is interesting but nonessential here. The chapter is crowded; a historical aside delays the real work without carrying weight.
4. **§2.3 / Line ~357** [DECORATIVE]: The agreement-and-gender material widens the topic just when the section needs to stay tight on determiner versus determinative. It feels appended rather than necessary.
5. **§3 / Line ~381** [LENGTH]: `Objects` is too slight to stand alone. It reads like a bridge section that has wandered into being a room.
6. **§4 / Line ~418** [SEQUENCE]: NP and AdjP complements arrive before modifiers get their full opposing role. These sections want to be closer together, with the contrast doing the ordering work.
7. **§5.2.1 / Line ~582** [DECORATIVE]: Measure phrases are a side chamber in an already full chapter. Cut, or fold into a sentence elsewhere.
8. **§6.6 / Line ~784** [DECORATIVE]: By the spray/load alternation, the chapter has already made its case for constructions. This is another specimen in a cabinet that is already full.

### Load-bearing
1. **§1.2** [EARNS IT]: Subject dropping turns a taxonomic section into a live pedagogical one. It reminds the reader that the grammar being described is used, not merely diagrammed.
2. **§2.2** [EARNS IT]: This is where the determiner/determinative distinction finally cashes out. Without it, the section is mostly setup.
3. **§5.4** [EARNS IT]: Integrated versus supplementary modifiers gives the modifier section real structural consequence. Before this, much of the section is inventory.
4. **§6.1** [EARNS IT]: Conditional coordination is the cleanest reason for bringing constructions into the chapter at all. It shows form-meaning pairing in one stroke.
