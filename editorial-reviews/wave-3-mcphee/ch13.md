## Fluency and flow

### Weight
This chapter is slightly overweight for what it has to carry. Its real load is twofold: fluency is not proficiency, and fluency competes with accuracy and complexity for attention. Once those beams are in place, the chapter circles them too often, especially in its second tour of the four skills and its repeated summaries.

### Structural flags
1. **Front matter / Line ~7** [RITUAL]: The learning objectives and the analytical-approaches box do nearly the same preliminary work. One can stay. Two delay the chapter's first true sentence.
2. **§13.2 / Line ~97** [RITUAL]: The CAF check box asks the reader to rehearse what the section has just said. It adds confirmation, not structure.
3. **§13.2.1 / Line ~107** [LENGTH]: The table earns its place. The prose after it largely restates the same distinction at greater length than the distinction can bear.
4. **§13.3.5 / Line ~210** [DECORATIVE]: "Putting It All Together" is a summary bridge placed immediately before another section that redoes the same survey. It is connective tissue with nowhere necessary to connect.
5. **§13.3.6 / Line ~224** [FURNITURE]: "Fluency across skills" repeats work already done in speaking, listening, reading, and writing. The Tokyo anecdote is useful evidence, but it belongs inside Listening Fluency if it is to stay.
6. **§13.4.1 / Line ~248** [LENGTH]: The catalogue of elicitation tasks has the feel of method furniture. The section becomes useful when it gets to rate benchmarks and what they mean.
7. **§13.5 / Line ~277** [LENGTH]: A section called "Factors affecting fluency" arrives thin. Either enlarge it so the heading is justified or fold these points into earlier sections where they can do more work.
8. **§13.6 / Line ~295** [RITUAL]: The chapter has already summarized itself more than once. This final list is tidy, but tidiness is not the same as necessity.

### Load-bearing
1. **§13.1** [EARNS IT]: The introduction establishes the chapter's actual subject: flow rather than general proficiency. Without that distinction, the rest of the chapter has no clean object.
2. **§13.2** [EARNS IT]: The CAF triangle supplies the mechanism. It explains why fluency must sometimes be taught by relaxing demands for accuracy and complexity.
3. **§13.3.2** [EARNS IT]: The listening section keeps the chapter from collapsing fluency into speaking speed. Its point about parsing the speech stream carries real weight.
4. **§13.4** [EARNS IT]: Measures give the chapter teeth. Rates and observable signs keep "fluency" from remaining a metaphor.
