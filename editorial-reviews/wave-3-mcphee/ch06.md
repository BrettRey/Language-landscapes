## Syntax trees

### Weight
This chapter is heavier than its subject requires. The load-bearing material is real, but the middle is overfurnished with serial demonstrations, repeated explanations, and boxed rituals. Cut roughly a quarter of it, mostly from the example galleries, and the chapter would stand straighter.

### Structural flags
1. **§6.1 / Line ~24** [LENGTH]: The introduction says three times what the chapter will do. One clean paragraph would suffice; the real case for usefulness is made later in §6.4.
2. **§6.3 / Line ~80** [DECORATIVE]: The solar-system metaphor lands head and dependent quickly, then keeps expanding outward to moons, galaxies, and black holes. The analogy outlives its use.
3. **§6.5.1 / Line ~167** [SEQUENCE]: This section invokes the checking procedure before §6.5.5 has taught it. The chapter reaches ahead, then doubles back.
4. **§6.5.2 / Line ~176** [LENGTH]: The top-down example spends six figures assembling one simple clause. The reader gets the method early; the later increments are mostly administrative.
5. **§6.5.3 / Line ~243** [LENGTH]: This is the chapter's heaviest patch. The repeated bullet pattern for *happy*, *very*, *about*, *it*, *about it*, and *very happy about it* keeps restating tree anatomy after the point is secure.
6. **§6.5.5 / Line ~411** [RITUAL]: The self-assessment box repeats the checklist in softer language. It feels assigned rather than necessary.
7. **§6.6 / Line ~418** [DECORATIVE]: "More trees" arrives after a long run of trees and mostly confirms what has already been shown. Its useful residue could be folded into the preceding section.
8. **§6.9 / Line ~487** [SEQUENCE]: The terminological dispute is not native to this chapter's main job. It belongs earlier, where categories and functions are first argued, or else in a note.

### Load-bearing
1. **§6.4** [EARNS IT]: This section gives the chapter permission to exist. Without it, the reader sees notation; with it, the reader sees necessity.
2. **§6.5.4** [EARNS IT]: Constituency tests are the chapter's practical engine. They convert tree drawing from diagram-copying into analysis.
3. **§6.7** [EARNS IT]: Ambiguity is where trees do work prose cannot do as compactly. This is one of the few places the chapter shows, not merely tells, why the tool matters.
4. **§6.8** [EARNS IT]: The argument for function labels justifies the book's chosen tree style with a clean contrast. It keeps the notation from feeling arbitrary.
