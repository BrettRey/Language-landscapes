## The Nature of Grammaticality

### Weight
This chapter carries more than its frame wants to carry. Through the definition of grammaticality and the account of how judgments go wrong, it is solid and forward-moving; once it turns into a broader survey of language change, the load spreads and the chapter goes a class heavier than it needs to.

### Structural flags
1. **§20.1 / Line ~24** [LENGTH]: The handball analogy earns the opening and then keeps speaking after it has made its point. By the later paragraphs it is no longer bearing weight; it is occupying space.
2. **§20.2.1 / Line ~98** [SEQUENCE]: The turn to domain-general biases arrives before the chapter has finished establishing grammaticality on the ground. This abstraction belongs later, with change, or not at all.
3. **§20.3.3 / Line ~199** [DECORATIVE]: "Judgment Severity Range" restates the chapter's argument in a softer key after the three-type framework and processing section have already done the work. Cut it and little structural force is lost.
4. **§20.4.2 / Line ~251** [DECORATIVE]: The subsection on domain-general biases broadens the scope just when the chapter should be narrowing toward its close. It reads like material from a neighboring chapter on change.
5. **§20.4.3 / Line ~269** [LENGTH]: Dependency distance is a real pressure, but here it is granted a standalone unit larger than its contribution. Fold its essential point into structural pressures and move on.
6. **§20.4.4 / Line ~327** [RITUAL]: The two boxed exercises at the end are workbook ceremony. After a long explanatory section, they add thickness, not leverage.

### Load-bearing
1. **§20.2** [EARNS IT]: This is the chapter's hinge. It replaces correctness with accepted form-meaning pairing and gives every later claim something firm to sit on.
2. **§20.2.1** [EARNS IT]: The community material keeps the argument from collapsing back into Standard-English moralism. It shows where grammaticality lives.
3. **§20.3.1** [EARNS IT]: The three-part breakdown of ungrammaticality is the chapter's main piece of usable structure. It turns proposition into method.
4. **§20.3.2** [EARNS IT]: The false-negative/false-positive distinction is necessary ballast. It separates grammar from the reader's processing failures and keeps the chapter honest.
5. **§20.4.1** [EARNS IT]: If the chapter is going to keep a section on change, this is the part that earns it. It shows grammaticality as historical pressure, not static rulebook.
