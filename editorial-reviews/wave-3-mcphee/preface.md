## Preface

### Weight
This preface is slightly over its fighting weight. It has the necessary bones, but it repeats its welcome and carries one note that belongs later, when the phonology chapter arrives.

### Structural flags
1. **§0.1 / Line ~3** [FURNITURE]: The opening mission statement announces the book in broad terms, but the next sections do the real orienting. Compress it to a sentence or lose it.
2. **§0.3 / Line ~7** [LENGTH]: The paragraph explaining the chapter order earns its place, but not all of its length. It repeats the roadmap more than it sharpens it.
3. **§0.5 / Line ~12** [DECORATIVE]: The paragraph about underrepresented areas, broad relevance, and common pitfalls feels like a second preface arriving late. Little structural weight rests on it.
4. **§0.6-0.7 / Line ~14** [RITUAL]: The closing encouragement and the one-line map metaphor are ceremony. Remove them and the structure stands.
5. **§0.8 / Line ~18** [SEQUENCE]: This is the real statement of necessity, the paragraph that says why this book exists and why CGEL matters. It wants to come earlier, before the roadmap, so the map has a reason.
6. **§0.10 / Line ~24** [SEQUENCE]: The Canadian English note is useful and misplaced. It belongs at the start of the phonology chapter, where the reader can use it.

### Load-bearing
1. **§0.2** [EARNS IT]: The chapter map tells the reader what territory the book covers. A textbook preface must orient, and this paragraph does.
2. **§0.4** [EARNS IT]: The modular reading paths respect how teachers actually use books. This is practical structure, not ornament.
3. **§0.8-0.9** [EARNS IT]: The statement of departure from traditional grammar and the note on the simplified CGEL framework are the foundation. Without them, the preface describes the book without naming its method.
