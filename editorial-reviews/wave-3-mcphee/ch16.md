## Pragmatics

### Weight
The chapter is slightly overweight for the load it needs to carry. Its main frame is sound: implicature, speech acts, politeness. What adds bulk are the textbook rituals and the late pedagogical appendices, which make the chapter explain itself more than it advances.

### Structural flags
1. **§16.0 / Line ~7** [RITUAL]: The learning objectives box is inventory, not structure. The chapter could begin with the opening exchange and start bearing weight immediately.
2. **§16.1 / Line ~61** [FURNITURE]: The Nation's Four Strands passage brings in a general teaching framework the chapter does not build on. It feels included because it belongs somewhere in the book, not because this chapter needs it.
3. **§16.2 / Line ~109** [RITUAL]: The first practice box interrupts the section before the idea has fully earned a pause. It performs the duty of a textbook, but not the work of this chapter.
4. **§16.4 / Line ~340** [LENGTH]: "Addressing the teacher" is a useful example that has overexpanded. The Ontario and LINC particulars narrow the chapter to one local case long after the general point is clear.
5. **§16.5 / Line ~360** [SEQUENCE]: The dedicated section on context arrives after context has already been doing the work of the chapter from page one. Here it reads as a return, not an advance. Move it forward or absorb it into the opening.
6. **§16.6 / Line ~405** [LENGTH]: The research-synthesis material on explicit versus implicit instruction, duration, learner level, and measurement slips into literature review. The chapter needs the consequence for teachers, not the full scaffolding.
7. **§16.6.1 / Line ~413** [DECORATIVE]: The AI subsection feels appended after the room is already furnished. If the topic must stay, it wants the size of a box, not the weight of a full subsection.

### Load-bearing
1. **§16.1** [EARNS IT]: The introduction defines the chapter's central distinction at once: literal meaning is not enough. Everything that follows depends on that first separation between semantics and use.
2. **§16.2** [EARNS IT]: Cooperation and implicature gives the chapter its engine. Without this section, later observations about indirectness and inference would not have a mechanism behind them.
3. **§16.3** [EARNS IT]: Speech acts changes the unit of analysis from sentences to actions. That shift is necessary; it lets the chapter move from interpretation to social effect.
4. **§16.4** [EARNS IT]: Politeness and face brings power, distance, and imposition into the structure. This is where the chapter's abstractions begin to matter in the classroom and in lived interaction.
