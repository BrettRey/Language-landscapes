## Negation

### Weight
The chapter has a real spine, but too much is strapped to it. It wants to be history, taxonomy, semantic analysis, pedagogy, and tree review at once. Cut the repeated instructional apparatus and trim the sections whose own conclusion is that little changes; the chapter would come down to the right fighting weight.

### Structural flags
1. **§11.0 / Line ~7** [RITUAL]: The chapter is boxed before it begins, then boxed again to explain the tests. One frame would suffice. Two opening rituals delay the first piece of actual load-bearing material.
2. **§11.1 / Line ~32** [FURNITURE]: The introduction is throat-clearing. Its work can be done by the first paragraph of the history section.
3. **§11.2.1 / Line ~182** [LENGTH]: Stages six and seven, especially the forecast about `anything` and `en`, run past the evidence. The history earns its space; the prophecy does not.
4. **§11.2 / Line ~237** [LENGTH]: The internal summary after Jespersen's cycle restates lessons the section has already carried. It feels like a conclusion before the chapter has reached its middle.
5. **§11.3 / Line ~249** [LENGTH]: The opening explanation of scope tours business, math, and programming before getting to language. One analogy is enough; the sentence pair that follows does the real structural work.
6. **§11.4 / Line ~302** [SEQUENCE]: The chapter classifies clausal and sub-clausal negation before giving the reader the polarity-item test that makes the distinction usable. The diagnostic should arrive earlier, or this section should be rewritten so it does not lean on a promise of explanation later.
7. **§11.4.3-4 / Line ~382** [DECORATIVE]: Affixal and pragmatic negation broaden the inventory but do not bear much of this chapter's weight. One belongs mainly to morphology, the other to pragmatics. Here they feel annexed.
8. **§11.7 / Line ~605** [LENGTH]: The phrase-structure section spends a good many pages establishing that negation changes very little structurally. If that is the point, two representative trees should be enough.

### Load-bearing
1. **§11.2.1** [EARNS IT]: Jespersen's cycle gives the chapter its backbone. It justifies why negation is worth a full chapter and ties the topic to change, grammaticalization, and learner error.
2. **§11.3.1** [EARNS IT]: The `can't`/`mustn't` contrast makes scope do visible work. It turns an abstract term into a usable distinction the reader can carry forward.
3. **§11.5.1** [EARNS IT]: The section on non-affirmative items pays off an earlier question and gives the chapter its best diagnostic tool. It is the point at which the analysis becomes operational.
4. **§11.6.3** [EARNS IT]: "Beyond simple polarity" keeps the chapter from stopping at a neat but false rule. It earns its keep by teaching the reader how to revise a generalization when the counterexamples arrive.
