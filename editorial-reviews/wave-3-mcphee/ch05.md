## Verbs, Tense, and Aspect

### Weight
This chapter is carrying more than one load. Its main beam runs from lexical vs. auxiliary verbs through aspect to the tense/time distinction and the system table; around that beam, other rooms have been built, and not all of them are needed. Remove one major annex, most likely complementation or the cross-linguistic material, and the chapter would stand more cleanly.

### Structural flags
1. **§5.0 / Line ~5** [RITUAL]: The learning objectives read like a contents list in bullet form. They announce breadth, but they do not help the reader understand order or necessity.
2. **§5.4 / Line ~220** [LENGTH]: The "What about gerunds?" box is a long detour in a section meant to move the reader toward tense and aspect. It may deserve a place in the book, but not this much space here.
3. **§5.5.1 / Line ~330** [LENGTH]: The progressive subsection makes its point early. After the first contrast and figure, the later special cases and timelines keep restating the same structural idea.
4. **§5.5.2 / Line ~450** [DECORATIVE]: The perfect section is over-illustrated. Once earlier situation plus later reference point is clear, the repeated timelines begin to feel like variants rather than advances.
5. **§5.6 / Line ~570** [SEQUENCE]: The cross-linguistic section arrives before the chapter has finished stating its English argument. It would do more work as a short aside after the two-tense claim, or it could go.
6. **§5.8.1 / Line ~720** [SEQUENCE]: "No tense" opens a new subject inside what is supposed to be a consolidating section. Non-finite forms belong earlier with verb forms, or later under their own heading.
7. **§5.9 / Line ~795** [SEQUENCE]: Verb complementation is not furniture, but it is in the wrong room. By the time it arrives, the chapter has already made nearly all of its tense-and-aspect case; this feels like the beginning of another chapter.

### Load-bearing
1. **§5.2** [EARNS IT]: The lexical/auxiliary distinction is the first beam. Without it, nothing later about NICER, modality, or do-support has a frame.
2. **§5.3** [EARNS IT]: NICER turns a category into tests. It gives the reader a way to identify auxiliaries rather than merely naming them.
3. **§5.5** [EARNS IT]: Aspect has to be given its own room. Without it, the later distinction between grammatical tense and semantic time will not hold.
4. **§5.7** [EARNS IT]: The tense/time distinction is the chapter's hinge. This is where the material stops being survey and becomes correction.
5. **§5.8** [EARNS IT]: The system table gathers the scattered parts into one usable structure. It justifies much of what comes before it.
