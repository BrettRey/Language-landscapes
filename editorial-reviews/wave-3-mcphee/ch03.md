## Categories and functions: An overview

### Weight
This chapter is carrying more than its title promises. The overview of category and function is the structural core, but the chapter then takes on a full lesson on adjectives, another on pronouns and determinatives, and then a fresh run at definiteness and possessive NPs. It feels less like one chapter than like the front half of three.

### Structural flags
1. **§3 / Line ~5** [RITUAL]: The learning-objectives box is long enough to announce the chapter's overcrowding before the chapter begins. It reads like a contents page for several units, not a clean statement of one chapter's work.
2. **§3.2 / Line ~23** [LENGTH]: The garden metaphor makes its point early. Peonies and roses do the job; mulch, insects, and the salamander are extra furniture once the distinction between category and function has landed.
3. **§3.2 / Line ~52** [RITUAL]: The early comprehension boxes repeat points still warm in the reader's mind. One might help; the pair adds bulk before the chapter has generated enough difficulty to need them.
4. **§3.2.1 / Line ~90** [LENGTH]: The long chain of noun-noun compounds proves abundance, not structure. After a handful of examples, the point is secure; the rest could move to an exercise without loss.
5. **§3.3 / Line ~274** [SEQUENCE]: The adjective section is not a brief application of the overview but a chapter in its own right. Once it expands into diagnostics, worked examples, and review exercises, the later sections arrive with less room than they need.
6. **§3.4.3 / Line ~583** [SEQUENCE]: Definiteness opens a new front too late. With the quotation, figure, and exercise, it feels like the start of another chapter rather than the closing movement of this one.

### Load-bearing
1. **§3.2.1** [EARNS IT]: This is the chapter's governing beam. Without the many-to-many relation between categories and functions, the later distinctions look fussy instead of necessary.
2. **§3.2.3** [EARNS IT]: The account of head, dependent, subject, determiner, modifier, complement, and object gives the reader the working kit. The book cannot proceed without this map.
3. **§3.3** [EARNS IT]: The chapter needs one extended demonstration of the framework in use, and adjectives are a workable proving ground. The problem here is scale, not presence.
4. **§3.4.2** [EARNS IT]: The separation of determinative from determiner does real corrective work. It clears a persistent confusion and keeps the chapter honest about the distinction it has promised to maintain.
