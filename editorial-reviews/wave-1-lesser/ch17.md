## Conversations

### Overview
This chapter is lively and mostly in command of itself. The colloquial register generally works: it keeps the material moving and often turns technical distinctions into plain English without strain. The trouble comes when the prose grows decorative or reiterative; the chapter has a habit of making a strong point, then explaining it again.

### Flags
1. **Line ~81** [TONAL SLIP]: "Sure. We're enacting every concept: turn-taking, repair, body language..." -- The opening device is clever, but here it starts naming the machinery too baldly. The scene works better when the theory is implicit.
2. **Line ~121** [THROAT-CLEARING]: "Take a moment to consider your last conversation." -- After the staged opening dialogue, this feels like a second start. The chapter can enter its argument more directly.
3. **Line ~133** [OVERWRITING]: "It's as if we're all playing jazz without ever having learned music theory." -- By this point you've already used fish in water and a flock of birds. One metaphor would carry more force than three.
4. **Line ~150** [OVERWRITING]: "This seeming paradox..." -- The paradox has already been made clear. This sentence announces a conclusion the reader has already reached.
5. **Line ~246** [OVERWRITING]: "But the contrast with the authentic version reveals how much is lost in the smoothing..." -- Sensible point, but the paragraph keeps restating the same contrast from several angles. It wants tightening.
6. **Line ~307** [THROAT-CLEARING]: "While it would be unrealistic to attempt to learn what conversational style to adopt in each situation..." -- The useful material begins in the next sentence. This lead-in is dutiful and slow.
7. **Line ~359** [DEAD]: "We find similar patterns with other grammatical structures, as in this example..." -- A purely transitional sentence that adds no pressure or music. Let the example arrive without introduction.
8. **Line ~720** [TONAL SLIP]: "Real conversations rarely proceed in straight lines; they meander like a river, carving side channels whenever the terrain demands." -- Too literary for the job. The image calls attention to itself and softens an otherwise crisp section.
9. **Line ~762** [OVERWRITING]: "This constant, often unconscious, management of face can lead to a significant gap..." -- The point is interesting, but the prose turns abstract and inflated. One plainer sentence would do more.
10. **Line ~850** [UNDERWRITING]: "These repair mechanisms serve multiple functions." -- What follows jumps quickly into theory and terminology. The reader needs one ordinary-language sentence here before "illocutionary force" and the rest.
11. **Line ~895** [DEAD]: "The opening meta-conversation between Jim and Nao demonstrated these principles in action..." -- By the summary, this feels obligatory. The chapter has already closed the loop.

### Protect
1. **Line ~125** [KEEP]: "Conversation is central to who we are, but like fish in water, we rarely notice its true nature." -- Memorable and economical. It opens the subject without pomposity.
2. **Line ~251** [KEEP]: "The question for teachers isn't whether to use simplified dialogues... but whether students also get regular exposure to the messier, more authentic patterns they'll encounter outside the classroom." -- This is where the chapter speaks with real authority: direct, practical, and exact.
3. **Line ~453** [KEEP]: "Even a grunt can be a complete TCU if it does recognizable work in context." -- Excellent sentence. It makes a technical claim instantly intelligible.
