## Preface

### Overview
An energetic preface, and a mostly intelligent one: it knows what the book is for and who it wants to serve. Its weakness is excess explanation. The chapter list, the justification of the chapter list, and the recurring terrain metaphor all do more work than they need to; the prose improves whenever it becomes plainer and more practical.

### Flags
1. **Line ~3** [OVERWRITING]: "It doesn't merely complement existing knowledge" -- The first sentence already establishes the book's ambition. This second sentence restates it in broader, blurrier terms.
2. **Line ~5** [OVERWRITING]: "We begin by building a clear and solid understanding" -- This paragraph turns into an annotated table of contents. A preface needs shape, not every stop on the tour.
3. **Line ~7** [OVERWRITING]: "This order is intentional" -- After the previous paragraph, the rationale for the sequence feels explained twice. Keep the governing idea, not the whole defense.
4. **Line ~11** [THROAT-CLEARING]: "Throughout the book, you'll find that it often prioritizes areas that are typically underrepresented in TESL training." -- Too much cushioning. The sentence wants to say, simply, that the book emphasizes neglected areas.
5. **Line ~13** [OVERWRITING]: "As you explore this linguistic landscape, remember that language is an ever-changing ecosystem." -- The metaphor has already been introduced; adding "ecosystem" muddies it and enlarges a point that should stay plain.
6. **Line ~19** [DEAD]: "This book focuses on providing the essential elements and ideas that teachers need to embark on their English-teaching journey" -- "Essential elements and ideas" is abstract filler, and "journey" is one metaphor too many.
7. **Line ~23** [OVERWRITING]: "Many of these will align with General American English, but I include features such as Canadian raising" -- The sentence loses control under the weight of its example. The point is straightforward and deserves a cleaner statement.

### Protect
1. **Line ~9** [KEEP]: "Cross-references can be used to find light prerequisites; otherwise, feel free to reorder to fit your cohort, time, and aims." -- Concrete, generous, and genuinely useful. It respects the reader's circumstances instead of grandstanding.
2. **Line ~15** [KEEP]: "This book maps the terrain; it's up to you to explore it." -- Clean, decisive closure. The metaphor finally earns its keep by being brief.
3. **Line ~17** [KEEP]: "Much of it is useful, and much of it is right." -- Fair-minded and confident. It grants tradition its due before challenging it.
