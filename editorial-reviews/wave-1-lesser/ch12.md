## Categories, functions, and constructions

### Overview
This chapter is lucid in patches and dutiful almost everywhere else. Its best pages are the ones on subject dropping and constructions, where the author trusts examples and lets the argument move; its weakest habit is to announce, recap, and apologize instead of simply proceeding. The colloquial tone generally helps, but every so often it reaches for a joke or a conspiratorial aside that blunts the point.

### Flags
1. **Line ~35** [THROAT-CLEARING]: "At the risk of sounding repetitive, I will reiterate that there are no perfect definitions..." -- This is an apology for the prose before the prose has begun. Cut the self-consciousness and give the claim directly.
2. **Line ~283** [OVERWRITING]: "Subjects exhibit a range of characteristics..." -- The section has already established all of this. The conclusion merely inventories its contents instead of sharpening the takeaway.
3. **Line ~328** [TONAL SLIP]: "This is sort of like an anthropologist realizing that some of these ancient arrowheads they'd known about for hundreds of years were actually spear points." -- The comparison is too cute and not especially illuminating. It feels imported from another voice.
4. **Line ~343** [THROAT-CLEARING]: "There are cases where there is a predeterminer modifier, but these are rare and I've never been asked about them by ESL students. Nevertheless, if only for the sake of completeness..." -- Too much hedging for a small exception. State the exception cleanly or leave it out.
5. **Line ~524** [OVERWRITING]: "While verb complementation may be more familiar to students, the complement system extends beyond VPs to other major phrasal categories." -- This whole concluding paragraph slides into generic textbook summary. It repeats rather than resolves.
6. **Line ~700** [TONAL SLIP]: "You probably wonder how think about it could possibly be conditional, and find the notion utterly preposterous." -- The mock astonishment is pushed too hard. The example itself is interesting enough without this nudge.
7. **Line ~766** [DEAD]: "Giving money and having it convey very different things, but give a try and have a try don't." -- The sentence tangles its own comparison. Instead of clarifying what "semantically empty" means, it momentarily obscures it.

### Protect
1. **Line ~270** [KEEP]: "It gives many students the idea that, to be polite, their English needs to sound stiff, unnatural, and disconnected." -- Precise, concrete, and humane. It turns an abstract pedagogical complaint into something the reader can immediately feel.
2. **Line ~694** [KEEP]: "It is the form - the grammatical structure itself - that says 'you', and even a slightly different structure ... means something different." -- This is the chapter at its most effective: a hard syntactic point made plainly and with real force.
3. **Line ~729** [KEEP]: "while syntactically the structure appears to be coordination ..., semantically the first clause is actually subordinate to the second." -- Clean, exact, and genuinely clarifying. It earns the abstraction.
