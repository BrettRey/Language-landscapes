## Coherence and cohesion

### Overview
One of the stronger chapters in the manuscript. The prose is most alive when you are puncturing classroom myths and showing how much coherence depends on inference rather than on visible glue. The trouble is slackness: several middle sections explain the same point twice, and the chapter would gain authority from a firmer editorial cut.

### Flags
1. **Line ~54** [OVERWRITING]: "The connection between teachers and students is so obvious that it literally goes without saying. Including the details in (c) could only imply something less than straightforward." -- After the first sentence, the point is made. The rest leans on it until it loses force.
2. **Line ~60** [DEAD]: "But establishing this foundation implies that a structure is to follow." -- Too abstract to do any work. Name the structure here or cut the sentence.
3. **Line ~120** [OVERWRITING]: "Notice how the different colours create distinct chains, each focusing on a key aspect of the Renaissance." -- The paragraph then walks through every colour, every underline, and the overall effect again. One pass would be enough.
4. **Line ~197** [OVERWRITING]: "It preserves Brett as the consistent topic ... It signals explicitly ... It respects the 'given' status ..." -- Three formulations of essentially one idea. The list feels dutiful rather than illuminating.
5. **Line ~235** [OVERWRITING]: "This shift can introduce a subtle complexity, requiring readers to integrate or reinterpret elements within the discourse dynamically." -- Inflated prose for a modest distinction. The textbook voice momentarily turns grandiose.
6. **Line ~300** [THROAT-CLEARING]: "It's important to note that these types of lexical cohesion often work together." -- Stock runway. The example has already shown this.
7. **Line ~458** [OVERWRITING]: "In summary, connectives are just one tool in the cohesion toolbox." -- The section has already concluded; this paragraph concludes it again.
8. **Line ~606** [UNDERWRITING]: "Languages differ in how much they leave to inference." -- An interesting claim that needs one concrete comparison. As it stands, it feels asserted rather than shown.
9. **Line ~624** [OVERWRITING]: "But despite these differences in specific devices, the underlying principles ... are remarkably similar across languages." -- By this point the chapter is re-covering ground. The ending wants a sharper landing on pedagogy, not another loop through earlier material.

### Protect
1. **Line ~108** [KEEP]: "While linking words can certainly be useful signposts, they are not the main drivers of coherence." -- Clean, corrective, and memorable. It clears away a classroom cliche in one stroke.
2. **Line ~303** [KEEP]: "Sometimes the strongest connections in discourse come not from what we say, but from what we leave unsaid." -- An elegant hinge sentence: simple, exact, and a genuine invitation onward.
3. **Line ~452** [KEEP]: "But if used simply as a way to dress up weak writing in academic cloth, connectives will impress few readers and fool fewer still." -- Sharp without being cute. The rhythm gives the sentence its bite.
